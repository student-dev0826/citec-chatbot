About CITEC

Established in 2019, the College of Information Technology, Entertainment, and Communication (CITEC) is one of the newest additions to UBLC. It originated as a small department offering the Bachelor of Science in Information Technology and played a pivotal role as one of UBLCâ€™s pioneer programs back in 2011. The introduction of the Bachelor in Multimedia Arts program paved the way for the establishment of this thriving college.

CITEC is committed to nurturing critical thinkers, fostering research-oriented minds, and empowering individuals with creativity and innovation. We aim to shape our students into skilled professionals who can analyze complex organizational challenges, design innovative solutions, and contribute to technological advancements in the fields of information technology, entertainment, and communication.

At CITEC, we believe in unleashing the full potential of our students through experiential learning. Our dedicated and highly qualified faculty members serve as mentors, inspiring each student to achieve their best. We provide an environment conducive to learning because, at UBLC, CITEC is where technology meets creativity.

Discover boundless opportunities for growth, creativity, and technological excellence at the College of Information Technology, Entertainment, and Communication. Welcome to a dynamic learning experience!

Capstone Project Requirements

Final year major project guidelines for CITEC students.

Project Overview

The Capstone Project is a culminating experience that requires students to design, develop, and deploy a comprehensive IT solution demonstrating practical application of skills learned throughout the program.

Required Deliverables

  • Complete System Design Document (SDD)
  • Working prototype or fully functional application
  • Comprehensive technical documentation
  • User manual and deployment guide
  • Project defense presentation
  • Source code repository with proper documentation

Example Project: AI-Powered Chatbot

Minimum Standards for Continuation

To ensure academic excellence and timely graduation, students must adhere to the following minimum standards.

RequirementMinimum MarkPolicy Detail
Minimum Passing Mark (Per Subject) 75% or 3.0 Any grade below this (e.g., 5.0) is considered a failure.
General Weighted Average (GWA) Minimum 2.75 Students must maintain the retention GWA per semester/year.
Major Subject Minimum Minimum 2.0 (85%) A higher minimum may be enforced for core subjects.
Maximum Failed Units Not more than 9 Units Exceeding this per academic year can lead to disqualification.

University Grading Scale

  • 1.0 - 97-100% (Excellent)
  • 1.25 - 94-96% (Excellent)
  • 1.5 - 91-93% (Very Good)
  • 1.75 - 88-90% (Very Good)
  • 2.0 - 85-87% (Good)
  • 2.25 - 82-84% (Good)
  • 2.5 - 79-81% (Satisfactory)
  • 2.75 - 76-78% (Satisfactory)
  • 3.0 - 75% (Passing)
  • 5.0 - Below 75% (Failed)

Important Note

These standards are designed to ensure that students maintain consistent academic performance. Students who fail to meet these requirements may be placed on academic probation, required to undergo counseling, or in severe cases, may face academic disqualification. It is the student's responsibility to monitor their grades and seek academic support when needed.
